About Me

I'm Andrew Harrison, a software and web developer currently living in Victoria, BC, Canada.

Over the past 5+ years I have established myself as a generalist with a web focus, thanks to my experience working for companies with a variety of environments and pursuits. I am comfortable with JavaScript, HTML/CSS, Java, PHP, and SQL; additionally, I have some degree of familiarity with shell scripts, Python, Elixir, C#, and several non-relational databases (MongoDB, Neo4J, and RethinkDB). This breadth of exposure has helped me excel at translating patterns for performant, reliable, maintainable software across languages and frameworks. I feel at home in an Agile environment with continuous integration and am proficient in version control under Git.

In addition to my technical abilities I am known for my exceptional communication skills, diligent attention to detail, and willingness to be a teacher or leader. I maintain a deep commitment to professional growth, constantly reading up on advances and issues in programming as well as engaging in hands-on experimentation.

At this time, I'm especially interested in working remotely. This isn't just out of convenience. I am a strong proponent of asynchronous workflows, a fairly aggressive practitioner of Inbox Zero, and a constant presence in IRC and Google Chat. Moreover, while I will be living in Victoria for a little while longer, eventually I will need to move (most likely within North America) in support of my wife's career; my ideal outcome would be to maintain continuity in my own career and stay with a great company through any relocations. Please note that I am a US citizen with an open work permit in Canada.

Personal Projects

MI5 in progress

MI5 is a web-based clone of the board game Scotland Yard. Apart from my enjoyment of the game itself, my motivation for this project was to practice working with some libraries that I was already using (Dropwizard, Google Guice, jQuery) and to gain familiarity with some new libraries (Neo4J, Atmosphere, Google Maps, D3). Although there is still significant UI work to be done – particularly the routes between points, of which there are many, each to be drawn manually onto a map of a city known for its winding streets! – the current codebase encompasses a fully functional API, push notifications, and game logic governed by analysis of the backing graph database.

Autodidact

I have recently spun up this repo to gather various toys or katas as I learn new languages and techniques. At present, the only project it contains is an exercise that I used for my first foray into functional programming with Elixir: a cryptogram puzzle which runs from the REPL. Since writing that exercise, I have proceeded to develop two other projects of a more serious nature in Elixir (whose code is unfortunately not available at present), namely an implementation of approximate distributed latent Dirichlet allocation and a text annotation tool for preparing custom corpora for NLP tasks.
